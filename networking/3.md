**Domain Hierarchy:** <br><br>
**The domain system is structured in levels where TLDs (.com, .org, etc.) sit at the top, second-level domains define the main site name (e.g., tryhackme), and subdomains (e.g., admin.tryhackme.com) extend it to organize or separate services.**
<br><br>
**Questions:** <br>
**1) What is the maximum length of a subdomain?** <br>
**-> 63**
<br><br>
**2) Which of the following characters cannot be used in a subdomain ( 3 b _ - )?** <br>
**-> _**
<br><br>
**3) What is the maximum length of a domain name?**
**-> 253**
<br><br>
**4) What type of TLD is .co.uk?**
**-> ccTLD**

**DNS Record Types:** <br><br>
**DNS records define how domain names map to resources like IPs (A/AAAA), other domains (CNAME), email servers (MX), and text-based data for verification or policies (TXT).**
<br><br>
**Questions:** <br>
**1) What type of record would be used to advise where to send email?** <br>
**-> MX**
<br><br>
**2) What type of record handles IPv6 addresses?** <br>
**-> AAAA**

**Makign a Request:** <br><br>
**When you enter a domain, your device checks cache first, then queries a recursive resolver which, if needed, contacts root servers → TLD servers → authoritative nameservers to retrieve the IP, caches the result (based on TTL), and returns it to your browser.**
<br><br>
**Questions:** <br>
**1) What field specifies how long a DNS record should be cached for?** <br>
**-> TTL**
<br><br>
**2) What type of DNS Server is usually provided by your ISP?** <br>
**-> recursive**
<br><br>
**3) What type of server holds all the records for a domain?** <br>
**-> authoritative**
